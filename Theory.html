Theory

Wind Turbine

Wind Turbine is a machine used to produce electrical energy. It is used to convert Kinetic Energy into Electrical Energy. The Kinetic Energy is obtained from wind rotating the wind turbine which then generates electricity.

Sizes of Wind Turbine

Small Wind Turbines can generate upto 10 Kilowatts (KW) of energy. They are used to fulfill needs of a single home.

Big Wind Turbines can generate upto 10 Megawatts (MW). They are used to fulfill needs of small towns, factories and power plants. Also they supply electricity to large electricity grids.

Applications of Wind Turbines

The Wind Turbines are used as alternate source of energy. We use wind power to produce electricity. This electricity can be used for our daily life use and for power plants. It is a cheaper and cost effective source of energy.

Types of Wind Turbines

Horizontal Axis Wind Turbines (HAWT)

These are the most common type of Wind Turbines. They consist of three blades. The blades operate in upwind direction. The turbine is pivoting on the top of the tower such that the blades face in the direction of the wind.

Vertical Axis Wind Turbines (VAWT)

These turbines are omnidirectional wind turbines. It means they operate in multi directions. The turbines do not point in the direction of wind to operate. These vertical turbines can utilize winds blowing from all directions. They consist blades which can rotate around a vertical pole. Common type of vertical axis wind turbines is “the Eggbeater-style Darrieus Turbine”.

How Wind Turbine Produces Electricity

The Wind Turbine has fan and blades. The blowing winds move the blades of fan. These blades of propeller of turbine turn the rotor. Then rotor will spin the electricity generator. This creates the electricity.

The wind turbine uses aerodynamic force to convert wind energy nto electricity.

This aerodynamic force is transmitted from the moving rotor blades. These blades are similar to helicopter or aeroplane motor blades. As the wind blows across the blades, the air pressure on one side of the blade is decreased. Due to the difference in air pressure across the two sides of the blade, the lift forces and drag forces are created.

The Lift Force is stronger than the Drag Force. As a result, the rotor is spin round at fast speed. This rotor is connected to the generator.

We can have direct connection, like in a direct drive turbine. Also, we can have shaft and series of gears connection, like in a gearbox turbine. They increase speed of the rotation. As a result, an electricity generator starts producing electricity.

Finally, the transformation of aerodynamic force into rotation of a generator produces the electric currents or electricity.
