Introduction

Non-renewable resources such as minerals and ores, fossil fuels, coal, petroleum, and natural gas are being depleted at an alarming rate to sustain the energy usage worldwide. This rapid increase in the rate of depletion could be correlated with the excessive utilization of these resources as the world is seeing one of the biggest technological breakthroughs in so many industries since the dawn of time and there are no signs of it slowing down.

Without taking any steps to slow down the ecological footprint, the earth would soon become inhabitable by humans as the biocapacity around the world reduces to nothing. With energy generation being one of the biggest contributors to the ecological footprint, it is only logical for engineers to develop renewable energy generation systems to replace non-renewable resources as to cut down the ecological footprint in the long term.

With more and more engineering students pursuing the direction of renewable energy, it is extremely important for these students to have an interactive lab for them to interact with and further their understanding. A web-based virtual lab would be ideal for this purpose as the cost of development could be kept at a lower budget, implementing functions that are relevant to the outcome of a particular module.

The motivation of this project is originated from the Capstone Module (EBGE 3123) and is jointly developed by 6 students of UOW Malaysia KDU pursuing different engineering majors. See About Us for more information about us!
